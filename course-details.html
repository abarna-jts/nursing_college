Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM)

The courses of 3.5 years duration, On successful completion of the course a Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) is awarded by the Government of Tamil Nadu Board for Nursing Education recognized by the Tamil Nadu Nursing Council & Indian Nursing Council, New Delhi. This course trains an individual in taking care of patients.

The basic eligibility criteria for GNM Admission is that students must pass class 12, with more than 40% having English as a subject of study. GNM Syllabus consists of subjects like Medical Surgical Nursing, Mental Health Nursing, Child Health Nursing, Midwifery, Gynecological Nursing etc. GNM 1st Year subjects are Bio-Science, Behavioural Sciences, Nursing Foundations, Community Health Nursing etc. After the GNM Nursing course, students can pursue a career in nursing such as Clinical Nurse Specialists, Legal Nurse Consultants, Forensic Nurses, Travelling Nurses, etc. Salary in GNM Jobs ranges from INR 35,000 to INR 42,000, which increases according to work experience.

BSc Nursing Course

BSc Nursing course is a four-year undergraduate study in Medical science. Candidates who have passed their Class 12 with Biology or a diploma in Nursing are eligible to apply for the BSc Nursing course. BSc Nursing course is an undergraduate programme that equips students to serve mankind via medical treatment.

On graduating with a BSc Nursing course, candidates can opt for job profiles such as Executive Administrative Assistant, Staff Nurse, Emergency Medical Technician, etc. The BSc Nursing course graduate can earn up to INR 5.1 LPA. Candidates can continue reading further to know more about BSc Nursing course details.

Msc Nursing Course

MSc Nursing is a 2 year postgraduate course in nursing, clinical speciality and clinical practise. MSc Nursing syllabus is designed to skill and educate students advanced knowledge in the field medical care and treatment. Students learn about nursing research & statistics, nursing management, pediatric nursing, cardiological nursing, medical-surgical nursing etc.

Students who want to take admission in MSc Nursing should have completed their bachelor's or undergraduate degree in BSc Nursing or Post-basic BSc Nursing with a minimum score of 55%. The ones with a post-basic degree should also have work experience of one year before or after post-basic BSc nursing.
